A recap of the announcements from the past week that affected me:

### Wednesday

**10:45 AM:** The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints makes several announcements:
- They will [not be admitting]
(https://newsroom.churchofjesuschrist.org/article/april-2020-general-conference-format-change) the public to the
biannual worldwide general conference held in April.
- Missionary training [will be done remotely](https://newsroom.churchofjesuschrist.org/article/covid-19-mtc-adjustment),
effective March 16, until further notice.
- Large gatherings of Latter-day Saints [are prohibited]
(https://newsroom.churchofjesuschrist.org/article/covid-19-impact-large-gatherings-latter-day-saints), effective March
16, until further notice.

**7 PM:** [Large gatherings are prohibited](https://newsroom.churchofjesuschrist.org/article/ces-guidelines-covid-19) at
all campuses sponsored by the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day saints

### Thursday

**3 PM:** Brigham Young University [temporarily cancels all classes](https://www.byu.edu/coronavirus) through
Tuesday, March 17 to resume online March 18 and encourages students to return home. This follows a
[recommendation by Gov. Gary Herbert]
(https://governor.utah.gov/2020/03/12/gov-herbert-announces-recommendations-to-help-slow-the-spread-of-novel-coronavirus/)
against gatherings of more than 100 people.

**6 PM:** The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints [suspends all public gatherings]
(https://newsroom.churchofjesuschrist.org/article/gatherings-worldwide-temporarily-suspended) of members worldwide
effective immediately, beating their own effective date for suspending large gatherings announced on Wednesday.

<br></br>

As COVID-19 wraps its way around the globe, out from the headlines and into my community, I watch the world grind to
an abrupt halt. Communities hesitant to lose business make mild precautionary restrictions before beating their own
deadlines with disruptive recommendations, cancellations, and closures.
